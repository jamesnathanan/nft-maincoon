Ownership Status of NFT Collection

resale of NFTs

An NFT is simply a cryptographically signed receipt that proves that you are the owner of a unique version of a work. NFTs are not synonymous with ownership titles of (copyrighted or otherwise) works.

NFTs are characterized primarily by their resellability. When it comes to NFT, the main concern is proving the authenticity of the underlying goods.

What is the legal position regarding the resale of NFTs (made without a copyright owner's permission)? Will such sales be treated the same as those made with counterfeit goods?

An NFT that represents ownership of specific copies of works must be lawful. Otherwise, any further sale or distribution of such works has been deemed an infringement, and accordingly, may result in infringement claims, takedown notices, etc. The NFT Link may be broken when this underlying work is taken down and deleted for these reasons. In such cases, the NFT may represent the proof of ownership of no longer available work. Therefore, it is imperative that the NFT seller and the right holder be linked to the content directly and should form an integral part of the NFT.
